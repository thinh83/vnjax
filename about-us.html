Our Organization

Serving the Vietnamese Community

since 1986

The Vietnamese Association of Jacksonville and Vicinity, Inc. (VAJV) is a civic organization founded in 1986 by refugees from Vietnam who came to Jacksonville after the Vietnam war.

Before 1975, there were only a few Vietnamese-American families living in Jacksonville. After the fall of South Vietnam to Communist invaders in April 1975, the first Vietnamese refugees arrived in Jacksonville after they had been rescued on the high seas by the American Navy and given entrance to the USA. From 1975 through the 1980s, more refugees aarrived, after unimaginable dangers and deprivations in the jungles, at sea and in South-East Asia refugee camps. During the first decade of their settlement in this town, they got together on spcial occasions such as The Lunar New Year’s Day to keep their tradition alive and to reminisce their old country. In 1986, they founded an association, the VAJV, to provide leadership in their community activities. The Vietnamese community of Jacksonville and its vicinities is thankful to the United States of America for having given them the chance to rebuild their lives.

The VAJV embraces and displays the flag of the Republic of Vietnam (South Vietnam) as the symbol of the ideal of Freedom and the quest for democracy for Vietnam. This flag, with a yellow background and three red stripes, has been officially recognized by many cities and states (including Florida) in the USA as the “emblem of the Vietnamese-American communities and their cultural identity, representing freedom, democracy and the resilience of the Vietnamese people”

Our Mission

The Vietnamese Association of Jacksonville is dedicated to advancing the Vietnamese American community in Jacksonville through direct services, advocacy, and education. Our approach includes but not limited to...

(1) Empowering low-income families and seniors through direct services and providing access to government and private programs.

(2) Advocating for social and economic equality by building awareness, capacity and solidarity within the community.

(3) Unifying multiple generations of Vietnamese Americans by promoting cultural traditions, history, art and language.

Our History

Started in 1986 by a group of volunteers to help with refugee resettlement, the Vietnamese Association of Jacksonville is the oldest Vietnamese American community based organization in Jacksonville.

VAJ has helped refugees and immigrants of all backgrounds secure housing, find jobs, study for U.S citizenship tests, and hosted U.S. Naturalization Ceremonies. We also organized annual cultural and community events such as the Tet Lunar New Year’s Festival and the Mid-Autumn Festival.

Our Successes

For almost 30 years, VAJ has provided culturally and linguistically appropriate direct services to the Vietnamese and other immigrant communities. Because of our reach in the community and our visibly, we also serve as a liaison for many agencies to ensure that vital information reaches our population. We attribute our success to our ability to anticipate the changing needs in the community and responding appropriately to these needs.
